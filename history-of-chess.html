Have you ever looked at your chess pieces and wondered where they came from? They didn't just appear out of thin air! Before there were video games or tablets, there was a magical game born right here in India that conquered the whole world.

üìú It Started with a Game Called Chaturanga

Long, long ago, in the royal palaces of India, wise people created a game called Chaturanga. In Sanskrit, it means "four parts." Back then, the game was a way for Kings to learn how to protect their kingdom.

Instead of the pieces we know today, the board was filled with a real ancient army: the Chariots (Rooks), the Mighty Elephants (Bishops), the Brave Horses (Knights), and the Foot Soldiers (Pawns).

üåç The Game Begins to Travel

First, travelers took the game to Persia. The Persians loved it! They started calling the King the "Shah." When the King was trapped, they would shout "Shah Mat!" That is where our word "Checkmate" comes from! It means "The King is finished."
