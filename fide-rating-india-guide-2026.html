Before you can play in any rated tournament, you must be a member of the All India Chess Federation (AICF).

  • How to Register: New players must visit the official AICF PRS Page.

  • Renewal: Membership follows the financial year (April to March).

Step 2: Understanding the "Three Flavors" of Ratings ‚è±Ô∏è

FIDE maintains three separate rating lists: Standard (Classical), Rapid, and Blitz. Standard ratings are the "Gold Standard" for serious improvement. Note that getting a rating in one category does not automatically give you a rating in the others.

Step 3: Finding the Right Tournament üèÜ

Look for authorized events on the AICF All-Events Calendar.

üíé Pro Tip for Parents: Before jumping into an official FIDE-rated event, have your child play local practice tournaments. Once they beat FIDE-rated players consistently in local games, they are ready for the international stage.

Step 4: The 5-Game Rule & 1400 Floor üî¢

To get your first rating in any category, you must meet these criteria:

  1. Play 5 games against players who already have a FIDE rating in that specific category.
  2. Score at least 0.5 points (at least one draw or win) against those rated players.
  3. Cross the 1400 Threshold: Since 2024, the "Rating Floor" is 1400. If your performance is below this, you remain "Unrated."
Technical Note: FIDE now uses a "Virtual Game" formula. Your performance is calculated based on 5 real games plus two "imaginary draws" against 1800-rated opponents to ensure rating stability.

Step 5: Tournament Survival Guide ü•§

  • The "Chess Diet": Pack fruit, nuts, and water. Avoid heavy meals between rounds.
  • The "Cool Down": Give your child 10 minutes of quiet time after a game before discussing the moves.
